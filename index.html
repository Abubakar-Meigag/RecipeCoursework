Active listening techniques

There is a big difference between hearing and listening, and listening is always more effective than hearing because it requires you to concentrate on the verbal and nonverbal messages being sent and to determine the meaning of those messages. At its best, listening is active, focused, and concentrated attention for the purpose of understanding the meanings expressed by a speaker. This points to the importance of effective listening: Effective listening is central to fostering interpersonal relationships with coworkers, leaders, and clients. It can affect one's relationship satisfaction and can be a determining factor in whether someone is an effective communicator. Effective listening can enhance your image as people will perceive you as intelligent and perceptive. Good listening can also help with your public speaking skills. As a result, you have the ability to analyze what you think works and doesn't work in others' speeches, which can help you improve your own speeches. Listening, in addition to other communication abilities, is a likely predictor of who gets promoted or receives other relevant rewards, such as status and power. Better listeners hold higher-level positions and are promoted more often than those with less-developed listening abilities. Finally, understand the components of being an active listener and critically apply them to your professional interactions. Realize your strengths and weaknesses as a listener and adapt accordingly
