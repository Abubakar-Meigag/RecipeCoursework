My Favourite Meal and how to make it and where it comes from

Meat Kabsa

Kabsa is the most popular dish in the Arabian Gulf, particularly in Saudi Arabia and Kuwait. It is originally made with long-grain rice and served with lamb, goat or chicken. Nowadays, there are various ways of preparing kabsa, which vary depending on the region and ingredients added, such as raisins or nuts. Kabsa competes with other popular dishes such as biryani and machboos, but kabsa remains unique with its delicious flavor and ease of preparation in a variety of ways.

Instructions for Delicious Meat Kabsa:

  • In a non-stick pot, heat the oil and add the sliced onions.
  • Fry until the onions turn golden brown.
  • Remove the onions from the pot and set aside in a bowl.
  • Add half of the onions and the rest of the ingredients except for the kabsa spice.
  • mix. Mix well.
  • Add 4 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  • Add the kabsa spice mix and mix well.
  • Add the rinsed rice and stir.
  • Cover the pot and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes or until the rice is fully

  • Serve hot.

Ingredients for Meat Kabsa:

  • 1 kilogram of meat, diced
  • 3 cups of rinsed and cleaned basmati rice
  • 3 large onions (sliced)
  • 1 tomato (peeled and seeded)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 cup of tomato sauce
  • 6 pieces of cardamom
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • 6 pieces of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 2 limes
  • 3 teaspoons of kabsa spice mix
